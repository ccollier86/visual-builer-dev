CLINICAL FORMULATION:

The patient presents with symptoms consistent with generalized anxiety disorder, with significant impact on occupational and social functioning. Her PHQ-9 score suggests moderate depression, characterized by pervasive worry and sleep disturbances. The interplay between her anxiety and social isolation is evident, exacerbating her symptoms. Her family history of anxiety and depression suggests a genetic predisposition, increasing her vulnerability to these conditions. Protective factors include her insight into her condition and willingness to seek treatment, as well as a supportive relationship with her parents. The patient's resilience and motivation to improve her situation are crucial strengths in her recovery journey.

RISK ASSESSMENT:

The patient denies any current or past suicidal or homicidal ideation, which is supported by her PHQ-9 response. There are no history of self-harm or suicidal attempts. Substance use is not a risk factor, as corroborated by her DAST-10 and AUDIT scores. Her support system includes her parents, who are available in crisis situations, providing a crucial safety net. The patient has developed a basic safety plan, though it may require further elaboration for comprehensive crisis management. Immediate risk is low, but ongoing monitoring is recommended to address any potential escalation in symptoms.

PROGNOSIS:

The prognosis for the patient is cautiously optimistic, given her insight and willingness to engage in treatment. Her motivation to address her anxiety, along with a supportive family, are positive indicators. However, the chronicity of her symptoms and financial stressors present significant challenges. The patient's previous response to CBT and medication suggests potential benefits if reintroduced. Ongoing engagement with her support network and addressing financial barriers will be essential for successful treatment outcomes. While uncertainties remain, the patient's strengths and determination provide a solid foundation for improvement.
